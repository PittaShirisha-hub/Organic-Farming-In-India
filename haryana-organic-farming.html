Organic Farming in Punjab

Organic farming in Haryana is a steadily growing movement aimed at promoting sustainable agriculture and reducing the harmful effects of chemical-based farming.

In Haryana, organic crops include basmati rice, wheat, moong, chana, mustard, and sesame, along with vegetables like tomato, spinach, and cauliflower, and fruits like guava and papaya. Farmers also grow turmeric, aloe vera, and tulsi as medicinal and herbal crops.

Highlights

  • Organic farming in Haryana is supported by schemes like PKVY and HAFED, encouraging chemical-free, eco-friendly cultivation. Farmers grow crops like basmati rice, wheat, pulses, vegetables, and medicinal plants with training from dedicated centers.
  • Government Support through schemes like PKVY and HAFED for training, certification, and market access.
  • Diverse Organic Crops: Basmati rice, wheat, pulses, vegetables, fruits, and medicinal plants.

Support Programs

Haryana promotes organic farming through schemes like PKVY, HAFED initiatives, and training centers like DUCEOF. Farmers grow organic basmati rice, wheat, pulses, vegetables, fruits, and medicinal plants with government and institutional support.
