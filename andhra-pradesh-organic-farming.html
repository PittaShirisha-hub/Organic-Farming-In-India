Organic Farming in Andhra Pradesh

Andhra Pradesh is a pioneer in natural and organic farming through the state-led Zero Budget Natural Farming (ZBNF) initiative. The program encourages farmers to reduce dependency on chemical inputs and focus on ecological and sustainable agriculture.

Crops like millets, organic rice, groundnut, pulses, and cotton are increasingly grown under ZBNF, impacting lakhs of farmers across the state.

Highlights

  • Over 6 million farmers trained in ZBNF practices.
  • Andhra Pradesh aims to become India's first fully natural farming state.
  • Increased soil fertility, reduced input cost, and better water retention.

Support Programs

The state government, in collaboration with Rythu Sadhikara Samstha (RySS), provides training, mentoring, and monitoring of organic practices. The program is globally recognized and supported by UN Environment.
