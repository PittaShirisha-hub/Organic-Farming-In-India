Organic Farming in Punjab

Organic farming in Punjab is a growing but still developing sector, especially important given the state's long-standing dependence on chemical-intensive agriculture. Here's an overview of the organic farming landscape in Punjab.

Organic crops grown in Punjab include basmati rice, wheat, pulses (moong, masoor, chana), vegetables (potato, onion, tomato, spinach), fruits (kinnow, guava, papaya), spices (turmeric, ginger), medicinal plants (aloe vera, tulsi), and oilseeds (mustard, sesame).

Highlights

  • Focus on Basmati Rice & Wheat: Major organic cereals grown for local and export markets.
  • Diversified Cropping: Includes pulses, vegetables, fruits, spices, and medicinal plants.
  • Soil Health Improvement: Restores fertility lost to chemical farming.

Support Programs

Punjab supports organic farming through schemes like Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY) and assistance from PAIC for certification, training, and market linkage. NGOs like Kheti Virasat Mission also promote organic practices through education and community support.
